# Project Omega: Autonomous Logistics Grid Specification

**Version:** 1.4  
**Confidentiality Level:** High

---

## Executive Summary

Project Omega aims to revolutionize urban freight distribution by deploying a decentralized network of autonomous ground drones (AGDs) coordinated by a central AI hive mind. The system focuses on "last-mile" efficiency, targeting a **40% reduction in carbon emissions** and **60% reduction in delivery times** compared to traditional courier vans. By shifting freight from roadways to sidewalks and bike lanes, Project Omega addresses urban congestion.

The initial pilot will launch in **Sector 7 (New Seattle)** in Q3 2025, chosen for its dense mixed-use zoning and modern digital infrastructure. Success in this sector will validate the economic viability of the "logistics-as-a-service" model before broader municipal rollout.

---

## System Architecture

The Omega Grid consists of three primary layers designed to operate in near real-time synchronicity:

### 1. Physical Layer

- **AGD-X1 rover fleet:** Six-wheeled, semi-autonomous vehicles
- **Features:**  
    - LiDAR  
    - Solid-state batteries  
    - Modular cargo bay (up to 50kg)  
    - Rapid reconfiguration for parcel lockers, hot-food storage, or medical secure-transport

### 2. Edge Layer

- **Smart charging docks** every 2km
- **Functions:**  
    - Robotic battery swapping  
    - Local diagnostic checks  
    - Mesh network nodes for continuous connectivity

### 3. Core Layer ("The Hive")

- **Cloud-based orchestration engine**
- **Technologies:**  
    - Deep reinforcement learning  
    - Real-time optimization (traffic, weather, pedestrian density, order priority)  
    - Predictive fleet re-balancing

## Hardware Specifications (AGD-X1)

- **Chassis:** Monocoque carbon-fiber reinforced polymer, aluminum sub-frame
- **Max Speed:** 25 km/h (sidewalk), 45 km/h (bike lane/roadway)
- **Battery Range:** 60km per charge (NextGen Solid State Li-Metal)
- **Sensors:**  
    - 360° high-resolution camera array  
    - Ultrasonic proximity sensors  
    - Long-range LiDAR
- **Processing Unit:** NVIDIA Jetson Orin Nano (on-device obstacle avoidance, pedestrian intent prediction)
- **Communication:** Dual-redundant 5G, satellite fallback

## Risk Analysis

- **Cyber Security:**  
    - High risk of signal jamming or "man-in-the-middle" attacks  
    - Mitigation: AES-256 encryption, localized "return-to-home" fallback after 5s connection loss

- **Regulatory:**  
    - City council approval for sidewalk usage pending  
    - Legal team drafting new "Personal Delivery Devices" (PDDs) classification

- **Weather:**  
    - Heavy snow/dense fog affect LiDAR  
    - Operations halt if visibility < 50m; rovers seek nearest Edge dock

---

## Conclusion

Project Omega represents a pivotal shift in automated logistics. By decoupling the delivery vehicle from the human driver, the largest supply chain bottleneck—labor availability and cost—is removed. Success in the Sector 7 pilot will pave the way for nationwide expansion by 2027, potentially reshaping urban landscapes to be more pedestrian-friendly and less reliant on heavy delivery vehicles.
